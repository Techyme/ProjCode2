BEST FREE AND PAID ENTERTAINMENT

By neighborhood, starting uptown.

The Apollo Theater.

Listen, dine and dance to some of the most famous singers, comedy acts and new talent around. It's at 253 West 125th Street btw. Adam Clayton Powell Jr. Blvd. and Frederick Douglass Blvd.

The Studio Museum in Harlem

Located at 144 West 125th Street, between Lenox Avenue (Malcolm X Boulevard) and Adam Clayton Powell, Jr. Boulevard; you'll find exhibits, a gift shop, and educational activities for all.

Bryant Park Movies.

If you enjoy old and new movies and enjoy picnicing with others on a lawn, then you will find this series of movies (from May until August) a delight. Showings begin at sunset and are preceeded by cartoons and movie trailers. The park is between 40th and 42nd Street, with the big screen on 6th Avenue. Bring a blanket and a way to find friends as it is usually crowed from 5:00 pm on.

The Shakespeare Festival in Central Park

Located in the heart of Central Park (around West 81st Street), the Delacorte Theater is host to the Public Theater's stagings of Shakespeare's works. Julius Caesar and Midsummer Night's Dream are on this summer's line up. Tickets are obtained by lottery, first-come-first-served basis, and voucher systems throughout the city and at the Public Theater. Needless to say, the wait for tickets is long; and performances are rain or shine.
